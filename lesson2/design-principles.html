Visual Hierarchy

Hulu

hulu.com

Websites that utilize visual hierarchy are designed in such a way that the viewer's eye is guided to portions of the page in order of importance. Hulu's website is a good example of visual hiearchy.

It is clear by the way the website is designed that Hulu's primary focus is to encourage the site users to sign up for the "Hulu + Disney+ Bundle". This bundle is center of the page with a large green button to sign up. This is followed by the "Hulu Disney+, Espn+" bundle with a less apparent white button to sign up. Lastly, viewers are encourage to click on the "Hulu only" link, which is small, white, and less obvious.

Clean Design

Jess Huff Crochet Patterns

jesshuff.com

When a website design displays white space and clean design, the site appears neat, tidy, uncluttered, and is easy to follow. White space can be a powerful tool in website design to keep designs simple and uncomplicated.

Jesshuff.com is a wonderful example of presenting white space and clean design. The layout of the page is simple and easy to follow. The use of white space makes it effortless to focus on the crotch patterns being advertised. The clean design also makes it unchallenging to find website essentials such as tutorials and FAQS.

Rule of Thirds

Breitling

breitling.com

When a website follows the rule of thirds, objects that are part of the design are placed on one of the four intersections or in a section of the rules of third grid.

Breitling uses the rule of thirds on its site by placement of objects and text in the website layout. The image of the watch and the text on the homepage are placed on either an intersection or a section of the grid. The placement of the image and the text makes the wesbite more visually interesting and appealing.
